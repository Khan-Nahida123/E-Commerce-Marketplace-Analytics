# Project Insights

---

## Section 1 — Customer Analysis

### Insight 1 — Orders per Customer

The analysis shows that most customers placed only one order.

This indicates low repeat purchase behavior across the platform.

**Business impact:**  
Customer retention is weak, reducing long-term revenue potential.

**Recommendation:**  
Introduce loyalty programs and retention campaigns to encourage repeat purchases.

---

### Insight 2 — Revenue per Customer

The analysis shows that a small group of customers generates a large portion of total revenue.

This indicates a strong revenue concentration among high-value buyers.

**Business impact:**  
The platform depends heavily on a limited customer segment.

**Recommendation:**  
Identify and protect high-value customers through VIP engagement strategies.

---

### Insight 3 — Top Spending Customers

The analysis shows that top customers spend significantly more than the average user.

This indicates the presence of a premium customer segment.

**Business impact:**  
High spenders are critical to revenue stability.

**Recommendation:**  
Create premium offers and exclusive benefits to retain top spenders.

---

### Insight 4 — Repeat Customers

The analysis shows that no customer placed more than one order.

This indicates extremely low customer retention.

**Business impact:**  
The platform operates as a one-time purchase marketplace instead of building long-term customer relationships.

**Recommendation:**  
Retention strategies are urgently needed such as loyalty rewards, re-engagement campaigns, and personalized follow-up marketing.

---

### Insight 5 — Customers Above Average Spending

Only a small number of customers spend above the platform average.

Most customers remain low-value buyers.

This indicates revenue is driven by a limited premium segment.

**Business impact:**  
Revenue depends heavily on high-spending customers.

**Recommendation:**  
Build loyalty programs to retain premium customers.

---

### Insight 6 — Average Order Value per Customer

Order value differs widely between customers.

Some customers consistently place higher-value purchases.

This shows strong customer spending segmentation.

**Business impact:**  
Premium customers contribute disproportionate value.

**Recommendation:**  
Introduce tiered offers to grow mid-level spending.

---

### Insight 7 — Customers per City

Customer distribution is concentrated in major cities.

Urban regions dominate platform activity.

This reveals geographic demand imbalance.

**Business impact:**  
Revenue growth is tied to key metropolitan markets.

**Recommendation:**  
Expand targeted marketing in high-density cities.

---

### Insight 8 — Customers per State

Customers are heavily concentrated in a few states.

SP dominates the customer base, followed by RJ and MG, while many states contribute very small volumes.

**Business impact:**  
The platform depends strongly on a few geographic regions.

**Recommendation:**  
Focus marketing expansion in low-penetration states to reduce geographic dependency.

---

### Insight 9 — Customers Who Gave Reviews

Only a subset of customers actively leave reviews.

Most buyers do not provide feedback, indicating low engagement after purchase.

**Business impact:**  
Customer feedback data is limited, reducing visibility into satisfaction levels.

**Recommendation:**  
Encourage review participation through incentives to improve product intelligence.

### Insight 10 — Customers with Above Average Rating

A group of customers consistently gives very high ratings.

These customers represent satisfied and loyal segments.

**Recommendation:**  
Retain highly satisfied customers through loyalty and referral programs.

## Section 3 — Logistics Analysis

---

### Insight 21 — Average Delivery Time

The platform takes around 12.5 days on average to deliver an order.

This suggests moderate logistics efficiency but not competitive speed for modern e-commerce standards.

**Business impact:**  
Faster delivery could significantly improve customer satisfaction and retention.

---

### Insight 22 — Delivery Time per City

Delivery time varies drastically across cities, with some locations taking significantly longer.

This indicates regional logistics imbalance.

**Business impact:**  
Certain cities face operational bottlenecks that require optimization.

---

### Insight 23 — Fastest Delivery City

The fastest delivery city completes orders in about 3 days.

This proves the platform is capable of high logistics efficiency under optimal conditions.

**Business impact:**  
Best-performing cities should be used as a logistics benchmark model.

---

### Insight 24 — Slowest Delivery City

The slowest delivery city shows extremely high delivery time compared to the platform average.

This indicates a logistics bottleneck in specific geographic regions.

**Business impact:**  
Delayed delivery reduces customer satisfaction and increases return or cancellation risk.

**Recommendation:**  
Investigate shipping partners and routing efficiency in slow-performing cities.

---

### Insight 25 — Orders Without Delivery Date

A significant number of orders do not have a recorded delivery date.

This suggests incomplete fulfillment tracking or failed delivery events.

**Business impact:**  
Missing delivery records reduce operational visibility and harm customer trust.

**Recommendation:**  
Improve delivery tracking systems and enforce shipment confirmation controls.

---

### Insight 26 — Late Deliveries

A large portion of orders were delivered after the estimated date.

This indicates systemic delivery delays rather than isolated incidents.

**Business impact:**  
Late deliveries damage brand reliability and customer retention.

**Recommendation:**  
Re-evaluate logistics SLAs and strengthen delivery performance monitoring.

---

### Insight 27 — Average Freight Cost

The average freight cost is relatively stable across the platform.

Shipping expense forms a consistent portion of total order value.

**Business impact:**  
Freight pricing appears predictable, helping maintain margin control.

**Recommendation:**  
Monitor freight inflation trends and negotiate bulk shipping rates to preserve profitability.

---

### Insight 28 — Freight Cost per Seller

Freight cost varies widely between sellers.

Some sellers carry significantly higher shipping burdens than others.

**Business impact:**  
Uneven logistics cost can create seller dissatisfaction.

**Recommendation:**  
Offer optimized shipping programs or centralized fulfillment to balance seller logistics costs.

---

### Insight 29 — Top 5 Cities with Longest Delivery Time

Certain cities experience extremely long delivery durations.

These locations represent logistics bottlenecks within the network.

**Business impact:**  
Customers in slow regions may develop negative platform perception.

**Recommendation:**  
Strengthen regional delivery hubs and local courier partnerships to improve service speed.

---

### Insight 30 — Delivery vs Review Score

Faster deliveries correlate with higher customer review scores.

Long delivery times are linked to lower satisfaction ratings.

**Business impact:**

**Recommendation:**  
Invest in logistics improvements as a customer satisfaction strategy.
